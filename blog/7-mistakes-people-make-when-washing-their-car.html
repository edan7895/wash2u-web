7 Mistakes People Make When Washing Their Car

Washing your car sounds simple. Water, soap, done… right?

Not really. A lot of people actually damage their car paint without realizing it. Small habits — wrong cloth, wrong timing — slowly leave scratches, swirl marks, and dull finish.

If you’ve ever wondered why your car doesn’t look as shiny after a wash… this is probably why.

1. Washing Under Direct Sun

This is super common in Malaysia.

You wash your car under hot sun, water dries too fast, and suddenly you get water spots everywhere. Soap also dries before you rinse it off.

Result? Stains and uneven finish.

At places like Wash2U Lotus’s Selayang or Rawang, cars are washed in shaded areas for a reason.

2. Using the Wrong Cloth

Old T-shirt, random sponge… yeah, don’t.

These trap dirt and drag it across your paint. That’s how swirl marks happen.

Proper wash uses microfiber towels. Big difference.

3. Skipping the Pre-Rinse

Some people go straight into scrubbing.

Bad idea.

Dust and sand are still on the surface. You’re basically rubbing it into your paint.

Always rinse first. Let water remove loose dirt before touching the car.

4. Using Dish Soap

This one… surprisingly common.

Dish soap is too harsh. It strips wax, protection, everything.

Your car might look clean, but long term — paint gets dull faster.

5. Washing in Circular Motion

Looks harmless, but it creates visible swirl patterns.

Better method? Straight lines. Up-down or left-right.

Less noticeable marks if anything happens.

6. Forgetting the Wheels First

People leave wheels last.

But wheels are the dirtiest part — brake dust, grime, oil.

If you wash them last, you might splash dirt back onto a clean car.

That’s why professionals always start with wheels.

7. Letting the Car Air Dry

Sounds easy — just leave it.

But this causes water spots, especially with Malaysia’s mineral-rich water.

Proper drying (with microfiber) makes a huge difference.

This is actually why many drivers just go to Wash2U instead — whether it’s Lotus’s Selayang, Lotus’s Rawang or YouCity Retail Cheras.

Not just convenience. It’s about avoiding these small mistakes that slowly damage the car.

So… Is DIY Washing Bad?

Not really.

If you do it properly, it’s fine. Some people enjoy it.

But most of the time, people rush. Or don’t have the right tools.

That’s when problems start.

FAQ (Car Washing Mistakes)

How often should I wash my car?

Usually once every 1–2 weeks. More often if you park outdoors a lot.

Is it okay to use just water?

Water alone won’t remove oils and dirt properly. You still need proper car shampoo.

Why does my car still look dull after washing?

Could be swirl marks, old wax gone, or improper washing technique.

Is professional car wash really better?

Usually yes — mainly because of proper tools, technique and consistency.

Do I need waxing after washing?

Not every time, but occasional waxing helps protect paint and maintain shine.

Final Thoughts

Most car wash damage doesn’t happen in one day. It builds up slowly from small mistakes.

Fix those, and your car already looks better without spending extra.

Or… just let someone else handle it properly.

Either way, now you know what to avoid.
